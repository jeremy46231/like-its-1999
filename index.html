What in the world is this?

Okay, so here is the deal. When you click that big button up there, a real live Windows 98 computer boots right up inside your web browser. It has got Netscape on it for surfing around, and EditPlus for writing your HTML, and Paint Shop Pro for making your graphics, and all sorts of other things that folks really did use back in the day.

What I would love for you to do is build a website on that old machine, and then send it over to me. The one big catch is that everything you use to make it has to be from before the year 2000. So no reaching for your fancy modern tools! It is just you and a beige box, putting a website together the way we all used to when the web was still young.

You Ship  ·  We Ship

» YOU SHIP
A whole website that you built and finished on the Windows 98 machine. It can be about anything you like, and it can be great big or teeny tiny, just so long as you made the entire thing in there with the old software. 		» WE SHIP
A real floppy disk with your very own code saved onto it, sent right to your mailbox. I will have a look at what you made first, and then I will pop it in the post to you.

The Rules

  1. Everything you actually RUN has to be from before the year 2000. The way I think about it, your browser and the emulator are the computer itself, and everything you build your site with lives inside there.

  2. You have got to build it inside the machine. Now, it is totally okay to use the real internet on your own computer to look things up and learn how stuff works. But your actual website has to be made in Windows 98.

  3. You have to ship a website that really and truly works. It needs to load up and actually do something. A page that only says it is under construction does not count, which, yes, I know is a little bit ironic.

  4. Have fun with it! Pretend it is the last summer before the whole world got itself into a panic about Y2K.
